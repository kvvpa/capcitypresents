**Clover Nahebedian
**

Clover (she/her) is a trans musician from Portland, OR by way of Rhode Island. She describes her music as 'seriously unserious.' From 2012-2018 she played in the art-punk band Dérive, who opened for Perfect Pussy, Pigeon Pit, A Great Big Pile Of Leaves, Empire Empire I Was A Lonely Estate, and Downtown Boys before calling it quits. Since 2018 she's fronted the dance-punk band Cheap City, who have played over 100 shows in the US and Canada, released four full length albums, and have recently opened for folks like Laura Jane Grace (Against Me). As a solo artist Clover has toured the US, playing solo songs, as well as solo renditions of Cheap City material. An audience member once described her opera October Surprise as "seriously deranged."

**Liv Louis**

Liv Louis is an Olympia-based experimental songwriter, producer, and multi-instrumentalist. They combine eccentric, poignant lyricism and a boisterous stage presence to deliver performances that evoke a combination of existential dread, rambunctious laughter, and bittersweet tears.

**Bambi Strong**

Bambi Strong and The Radical Joy Project are a group of soft individuals that weave together
when the vibe is right towards the shared goal of helping people put words to the feelings in
their chest. Bambi’s melodic lyricism comes from a place of deep sorrow and deeper
compassion. Her main goal as an artist in all the forms that takes is to lead by example; being
radically joyful and kind, holding space for the ambiguous grief in their communities, Choosing
to Live every single day. We are so seldom shown the kindness we deserve. Queerness and
Joy and Liberation all exist hand in hand to give a middle finger to our collective oppressors!
Fuck you! We’re gonna THRIVE!!!!!!
The Radical Joy Project is a loose band of folks led by Bambi, who calls it a Project to make it
more equitable to everyone’s needs. Sometimes the Project is just Bambi! And sometimes it’s
more <3 But at every stage it’s something special to behold.
“Thank you for spending time with me”
~Bambi
